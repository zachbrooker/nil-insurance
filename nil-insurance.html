Coverage / 001 / NIL Insurance · Anchor product

Name, Image & Likeness insurance for athletic departments.

Protect the financial commitments tied to student-athlete NIL agreements and revenue-share allocations against covered injury or medical loss. Underwritten through Lloyd's of London syndicates specializing in contingency and performance-based risk.

The exposure / 001

A new category of financial risk.

NIL agreements have grown in size and complexity. Post-House v. NCAA, athletic departments now manage direct revenue-share allocations alongside collective-funded arrangements. Each contract is a financial commitment that exists independent of whether the athlete remains available to compete.

When a contracted athlete is injured or medically unable to participate, the obligation doesn't disappear — but the program loses the performance it paid for. That gap is exactly what this coverage is structured to address.

Scope / What responds

Injury & illness coverage.

// Trigger

Medical inability to participate

  • An insured athlete becomes medically unable to participate in team activities or competition
  • The condition is a covered bodily injury, sickness, or disease
  • Inability is certified by a licensed physician
  • The disabling event occurs within the policy period
  • Subject to the agreed elimination period and policy terms
// Response

Reimbursement of NIL commitments

  • Reimburses the athletic department or collective for the portion of NIL compensation still owed under contract
  • Applies to losses directly resulting from the covered injury or medical condition
  • Payment structure may mirror NIL contract terms on a reducing basis
  • Designed to preserve institutional budget certainty
  • Applicable across regular season and postseason commitments
Structure / 002

Coverage built around your program.

  1. / 01

    Single-athlete or group basis

    Policies can be structured for individual high-value athlete agreements, specific position groups, or roster-wide programs. Coverage aligns with how your NIL commitments are actually organized.

  2. / 02

    Shared aggregate limit

    The program provides a shared aggregate limit — a pooled reserve that responds to eligible losses from covered on-field, on-court, or medical injuries during the policy term.

  3. / 03

    Schedule mirrors contract terms

    Coverage is triggered based on time missed due to covered injury or illness. Policy language mirrors your NIL contract structure and may reduce as obligations are paid out, keeping exposure alignment across the term.

  4. / 04

    Approved funding sources

    Structure accommodates NIL obligations funded through institutional revenue-share allocations, donor contributions, and other approved collective funding sources.

Scope limitation / 003

What this coverage doesn't do.

This is injury-response coverage — not a general NIL wrapper. Understanding what's out of scope is as important as knowing what responds.

// Not covered

Out of scope

  • Transfers to other programs
  • Performance-related roster decisions
  • Disciplinary actions
  • Eligibility issues
  • Contract termination unrelated to covered medical injury
  • Losses outside the defined policy period
Underwriting / 004

What underwriters need to quote.

Placement runs through specialty excess & surplus carriers — primarily Lloyd's of London syndicates that underwrite contingency and performance-based risk. We coordinate the full submission.

  1. / 01

    Schedule of athletes & insured values

    A list of the athletes to be covered with their corresponding NIL contract values. Coverage can be structured as individual policies or on a roster basis.

  2. / 02

    NIL contracts & payment structures

    Underlying NIL agreements, including payment schedules, injury provisions, and any performance-related clauses. Policy terms align with contract language.

  3. / 03

    Athlete eligibility & medical certification

    Year of eligibility and, where required, certifications of health from team physicians. Medical documentation supports underwriting review.
