The exposure / 001

Success has a price.

Performance-based incentives are embedded across modern athletic contracts — coaching agreements, sponsorship deals, event contracts, and increasingly, athlete compensation structures. They drive results. But when performance targets are actually met, the resulting financial obligation can be significant and concentrated.

Contractual Bonus Insurance transfers that success-based risk to the insurance market. The policy pays out when the performance trigger is hit — so the program captures the win without absorbing the full budgetary impact.

Scope / What responds

Performance bonus coverage.

// Trigger

When the policy responds

  • Agreed-upon performance targets, milestones, or achievements are met
  • Performance criteria are clearly defined and measurable in the underlying contract
  • Event occurs within the policy period
  • Subject to contract-specific verification requirements
// Response

What the policy pays

  • Covers contractual obligations tied to clearly defined and measurable performance criteria
  • Mitigates financial volatility associated with success-driven payouts
  • Supports disciplined financial planning and liability management
  • Allows programs to deploy performance-based compensation with confidence
Structure / 002

Coverage aligned with contract language.

  1. / 01

    Single-contract or aggregate basis

    Coverage can be written for a single contractual obligation or structured as an aggregate program covering multiple performance-triggered payouts within a defined period.

  2. / 02

    Limits mirror bonus exposure

    Policy limits are aligned directly with the contractual bonus exposure. If the bonus is $500K, the coverage is sized to that obligation — no under- or over-insurance of the underlying risk.

  3. / 03

    Policy terms mirror contract language

    Policy wording follows the performance triggers, measurement criteria, and payout conditions in the underlying contract. Coverage responds the way the contract expects it to.

  4. / 04

    Flexible policy periods

    Terms are set to match the duration of the underlying incentive arrangement — a single season, a multi-year coaching contract, or the life of a sponsorship deal.

Underwriting / 004

What underwriters need to quote.

Placement runs through specialty contingency markets — primarily Lloyd's syndicates experienced with performance-triggered risk. Each submission is structured around the probability of the specific performance event occurring.

  1. / 01

    Contract & performance triggers

    The underlying contract language defining the performance condition — what constitutes the trigger, how it's measured, and when the obligation is due.

  2. / 02

    Historical performance context

    Relevant historical data on the performance criteria — win-loss records, statistical trends, or prior outcomes — so underwriters can model the probability.

  3. / 03

    Bonus amount & payout structure

    The financial magnitude of the obligation and any layering (e.g., tiered payouts for different achievement levels).
