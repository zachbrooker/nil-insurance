The use case / 001

A high-value prize, at a defined cost.

Athletic departments, collectives, and event organizers run golf tournaments, donor galas, and fan promotions constantly. A hole-in-one contest with a car, a halftime half-court shot for cash, a fundraising raffle — these drive engagement and fundraising.

But the financial exposure is skewed: probability of a prize being won is low, value if it is won can be substantial. Prize Contest Insurance flips that risk to the insurance market. The event pays a small, predictable premium. If the prize is won, the insurer pays out — not the program.

Scope / Coverage

Event-specific prize protection.

// Events covered

Where the policy applies

  • Organized golf events, including professional and Pro-Am formats
  • Hole-in-one contests at donor or fundraising outings
  • Skill-based prize contests and promotional challenges
  • Live event promotions at sporting events
  • Fundraising challenges tied to defined outcomes
// Response

When coverage pays out

  • Responds only if a participant successfully completes the predefined challenge under agreed-upon conditions
  • Eliminates uncertainty associated with high-value, low-probability outcomes
  • Pays the prize value directly, preserving program budget
  • Event pays only the premium — not the prize itself
Limits / 002

Insurable prizes & coverage limits.

// Insurable prizes

What can be covered

  • Cash prizes
  • Merchandise
  • Vehicles (luxury automobiles, trucks, motorcycles)
  • Equivalent-value awards (travel, memorabilia, experiences)
// Coverage limits

Prize values supported

  • Nominal amounts up to $1,250,000
  • Coverage terms tailored to contest mechanics
  • Probability and number of attempts factored into pricing
  • Hole distance and skill requirements reflected in policy terms
Structure / 003

How the policy is set up.

  1. / 01

    Event-specific, short-term coverage

    Policies are written for the specific event — a single tournament, gala, or promotion. Coverage period covers only the window of the event itself.

  2. / 02

    All contest rules defined prior to inception

    Coverage parameters — who can participate, what constitutes a winning outcome, how the attempt is verified — are all locked in before the policy binds. No ambiguity at the moment of truth.

  3. / 03

    Terms reflect contest conditions

    Policy language incorporates the specifics of each event: hole distance for golf contests, skill requirements, number of participants, and any contest conditions that affect probability.

  4. / 04

    Transparent, cost-effective risk transfer

    Designed to deliver a clear, fixed-cost solution. You know the premium before the event starts; the insurer absorbs the upside risk of the prize being won.

Examples / 004

Common athletic department use cases.

// 01

Donor golf outings

Athletic department golf tournaments with a hole-in-one prize — typically a car donated or leased by a local dealer sponsor. Coverage protects the department from the prize payout if a participant actually makes the shot.

// 02

Halftime promotions

Half-court shots, putting contests, or skill challenges during game breaks. Often sponsor-tied, with cash prizes ranging from $10,000 to $1M+. The program offers the engagement; the insurer covers the unlikely payout.

// 03

Fundraising events

Galas, dinners, and donor events featuring a high-value raffle or challenge prize. Coverage allows the event to promise a significant award while keeping the fundraising yield predictable.
